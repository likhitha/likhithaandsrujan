Likhitha & Srujan

Our happily ever after.

His Story

It all began one fateful afternoon at the Computer Engineering Lab at Virginia Tech. I was a senior working late on a design project and she was a sophomore there to see a TA (Teaching Assistant). I took an immediate notice of her as she walked in and took a seat, and I knew I had to go talk to her but I had no idea how to approach her.

Conflicted at first, I finally got up, walked over, and said hi. She said hi back. We made small talk, and the conversation grew effortlessly. It took a few minutes before I realized that she thought that I was the lab TA there to help her with her project! So I went along with it, knowing well that I have no idea what her assignment was about.

From that botched homework assignment, we went from acquaintances running into each other on campus, to good friends who shared a surprising amount of things in common, to something much much more.

Since that very day, I knew there was something special about her and little did I know then how far we would have come together within the next few years.

Her Story

I think it's mostly fortunate serendipity that brought Srujan and I together. I always fondly look back to our funny encounter at the CEL lab and chuckle to myself. I don't know what made Srujan come up to me that day, but I'm glad he did. We ran into each other on campus several times that year, and after many hilarious encounters, we eventually grew to become really good friends.

The Virginia Tech days ended, and I knew I was headed to Seattle after graduation. Coincidentally, a few months after I moved to Seattle, Srujan told me that he's moving to Seattle to work for a start up in the area! This was a monumental change in both of our lives and this is when we grew from good friends to much more.

We explored the beautiful pacific northwest, made amazing new friends together, inspired each other, and truly learned to be partners along the way. Sitting on the ledge by Kerry Park, overlooking the beautiful Seattle city, one day, we talked about our life ahead, and things felt right. I knew that day that I wanted to spend the rest of my life with him.

The Proposal

Getting married is one of those few, life changing moments, where everything is supposed to be different afterwards. I don't really see it that way. I wanted to marry Likhitha because I wanted more of the same things. More of our life together.

My planning for the big day started off, like all things millennial, with a WhatsApp group chat amongst our closest friends in Seattle. We used that to discuss every aspect and detail of the proposal from props and candle arrangements to how we will be standing on the day of. The weeks prior to the proposal day, we even met regularly to practice singing a song together -- Falling in Love at a Coffee shop by Landon Pigg, her favorite.

May 13th, 2015 finally arrived. I took the whole day off to pick up flowers, props, and prepare my sppech. I was feeling very nervous. I wanted it all to go perfectly. The plan was simple, the girls would take Likhitha to a restaurant off the pier while the guys and I setup and wait at Myrtle Edwards Park nearby. Then they would pretend to want to get dessert at a friends’ place, start walking through the park to where we are, we would all start singing, I would finally ask her the question. Even with meticulous planning, things didn't go as smoothly. As we were all setting up at the park, I was deep in thought as I was going over the lines of the song as well as my speech. When I reached into my pocket for my phone, and after checking across all my pockets, I realized I forgot the ring!!! A look of sheer horror came across my face as I started retracing my steps. My friend reassured me that it had to be in his car so we both bolting across the park while frantically texting the girls to stall at the restaurant. Thankfully the ring was in the car and by the time we rushed back to the park we were both completely out of breath. Then Likhitha and the girls arrived, I was calm after seeing her, we all sang, I asked her to marry me. SHE SAID YES!

Looking back, I still can't believe how magical that day was, how well everything fell into place, and I can't imagine how I would have been able to pull it all together without the help of our wonderful friends in seattle.
